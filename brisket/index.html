Brisket

Brisket is the brainchild of Moshe Berman. For reasons unknown to the general public, the hashtag #IfTheMovieWereJewish began trending in the United States on or around December 25, 2013. People were taking the names of popular movies and sharing parodies that sounded oddly Jewish.

The Problem: The problem was that as the number of parodies grew into the thousands, the references became increasingly obscure. I set out on a mission to clarify which movies people may have been talking about. Introducing Brisket.

The Solution: Brisket is a classic mashup, using Twitter's public API, and an undocumented API from IMDb, to work its magic. With the power of Twitter's hashtag filtering and IMDb's smart and powerful search, Brisket was just a matter of hours of hacking in Python.

How It Works Brisket first goes to the Twitter API and pulls roughly 9.5K tweets containing the hashtag #ifthemoviewerejewish. Then it burns away the fat, including retweets or tweets are longer than 6 words (after hashtags and mentions are discounted). Brisket also attempts to filter tweets with links, since those usually aren't relevant. Next, we hit IMDb for each of the remaining (roughly) 4-5K tweets, searching for movies. Before presenting, we filter the tweets that came back with nothing from IMDb. The remaning Tweets are presented along with author information, IMDb's results, as well as a unix timestamp. (I couldn't get JavaScript to format all of results nicely quickly enough.)

Special Thanks I'd like to thank Charles Carr for hanging out via Google Hangouts while I cooked up large portions of Brisket. Having a fresh set of eyes enforced my learning process and caused me to consider things more deeply.

Your Own Brisket: The source code and setup instructions is on GitHub. If you like this, please follow me on Twitter.
